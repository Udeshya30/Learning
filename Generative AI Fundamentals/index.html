Generative AI Fundamentals

Complete beginner-to-intermediate handbook covering Generative AI, LLMs, Transformers, Embeddings, Prompts, RAG, and AI Agents.

1. What is Generative AI?

Generative AI creates new content such as text, images, code, audio, and video. It learns patterns from large datasets and generates human-like outputs.

Generative AI is not magic. It is built using mathematics, statistics, machine learning, and neural networks.

2. Why Generative AI Matters

  • Content creation
  • Code generation
  • Customer support
  • Research assistance
  • Automation
  • AI Agents

6. Tokenization

LLMs do not directly understand words. They process tokens.

Sentence: I heard a dog bark
Tokens: [I] [heard] [a] [dog] [bark]

7. Token IDs

TokenID
I1
heard2
dog4

8. Vectors

Tokens are converted into numbers.

dog → [2,7,11]

9. Embeddings

Embeddings are vectors containing semantic meaning.

dog   → [10,3,2]
puppy → [5,3,2]
cat   → [10,3,1]
Similar meanings create similar embeddings.

10. Positional Encoding

Provides word order information to the model.

Dog bites man ≠ Man bites dog

11. Transformer Architecture

The transformer is the foundation of modern LLMs.

ComponentPurpose
EncoderUnderstand context
DecoderPredict next token

12. Encoder

Creates contextual embeddings using attention mechanisms.

13. Decoder

Predicts the most likely next token in a sequence.

"When my dog was a..." → puppy

14. Attention Mechanism

Attention identifies which words are most important.

I heard a dog bark
Important → heard, dog

15. Multi-Head Attention

Examines multiple relationships simultaneously, improving understanding and efficiency.

16. Cosine Similarity

Measures how semantically similar two embeddings are.
